PART 4: METHODOLOGY & RESEARCH FRAMEWORK

Short selection criteria

  • Fuel cost sensitivity: Companies where fuel/energy represents 15%+ of operating costs and hedging is inadequate
  • Gulf route dependency: Operations or revenue streams requiring >10% of capacity through Gulf/Hormuz/Suez corridors
  • Supply chain input vulnerability: Companies sourcing >30% of critical inputs from Gulf region (helium, sulfur, fertilizers)
  • Insurance/reinsurance war-zone liability: Direct exposure to energy facility or aviation war claims in Gulf
  • Consumer demand collapse: Tourism, discretionary spending in Gulf-affected markets
  • Valuation screen: Preference for companies already trading at elevated multiples that cannot sustain earnings downgrades

Buy selection criteria

  • US domestic oil production advantage: Low breakeven ($35–55/barrel) vs current Brent ($104+) = exceptional free cash flow generation
  • Defence contract backlog visibility: Multi-year programmes with sole-source contracts immune to budget cuts in a security-elevated environment
  • Gold/safe-haven demand: Structurally elevated by dual driver of geopolitical risk + central bank buying
  • US domestic supply chain immunity: Food, fertilizer producers immune to Gulf disruption gaining pricing power relative to disrupted imports
  • Energy independence narrative: Government policy acceleration of renewables — structurally important over 6–12 month horizon
  • Revenue model resilience: MLP pipeline structures earning on volume; streaming companies with no operational risk; SaaS defence tech with recurring contracts
